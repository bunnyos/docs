bunnyOS asks you to sign in before you can chat. Signing in gives you an account, and everything you do is saved to it.

<Frame caption="The sign-in screen you see on first load.">
  <img src="/images/app/sign-in.png" alt="The Sign in to bunnyOS screen with a Sign in to continue button." />
</Frame>

## Sign in on first load

When you open the app, you see a full-screen **Sign in to bunnyOS** prompt. Nothing else works until you sign in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Sign in to continue">
    This opens the sign-in window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose how you want to sign in">
    You can use a crypto wallet, an email address, or a social account such as Google.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finish the sign-in">
    Complete the prompts for the method you picked. You land straight in the chat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>Sign-in is handled by Privy, a trusted sign-in service. A wallet is just one of the ways to sign in — you do not need one to use bunnyOS.</Note>

## What's tied to your account

Signing in creates your account and saves your work to it. Your chats, settings, connected apps, Carrots, workers, linked wallets, and generated images all follow you when you sign in on another device.

A few preferences stay on the device you set them on, so they are not carried across:

- Which chat is currently open.
- Which built-in tools you have toggled off.
- Whether interface sounds are on.

## Manage your account

To see your account or sign out, open **Settings** › **Account**. It shows the email on your account, and it is where you end your session when you want to sign out.
