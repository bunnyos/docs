Before you size a long or short, ask the agent for a technical read on the pair. It looks at the live price and recent price action, then replies with a plain-language report: where the market is leaning, the levels that matter, and the risks to watch. It is research, not a recommendation: the agent never opens a trade for you.

<Note>
A technical-analysis (TA) report is read-only: it reviews the market and reasons over it, but never signs or sends anything. When you are ready to act, you set up and approve the trade yourself. See [Trade perpetual futures](/user/perps/trading).
</Note>

## Generate a TA report for a pair

The fastest way to get a report is from the **Perps** page, on the pair you already have selected.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the pair">
    In the **markets** panel, pick the pair you want from the **pair** dropdown, for example `ETH/USD` or `BTC/USD`. The whole page, including the chart, follows your selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click prep in chat">
    Above the price chart, click **prep in chat**. This opens the agent chat and automatically asks it to analyze the selected pair, so you do not have to type anything. You can also just ask in chat: for any listed pair, type something like "give me a technical read on `BTC/USD`" and the agent runs the same analysis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the report in the conversation">
    The agent runs the analysis and replies with a structured read on the pair. Follow up in plain language, ask about a specific level, or how a long would sit against the current market.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the report includes

Expect to see:

- **Directional bias**: whether the setup leans bullish, bearish, or neutral, based on how the price is trending.
- **Support and resistance levels**: the key prices to watch, drawn from recent highs, lows, and trends.
- **Positioning**: how the market is leaning long versus short, which is part of the risk picture. A heavily one-sided market can lead to sharp moves.
- **Cost to hold**: the ongoing cost of keeping the position open, showing which side is paying to stay in the trade.
- **Risk summary**: what could go wrong, such as stretched momentum, crowded positioning, or volatility that widens your liquidation distance.

<Warning>
A TA report is a snapshot of probabilities, not a prediction. Markets move against strong-looking setups, and leverage magnifies every move. Treat it as one input, size conservatively, and never risk USDC you cannot afford to lose.
</Warning>

The **Perps** page also shows a live price chart for the selected pair, so you can eyeball the levels the agent calls out. See [Trade perpetual futures](/user/perps/trading).
