---
title: "Research token security & generate reports"
description: "Generate an AI research report covering security, holders, and market data for any Base token."
---

The **Research** tab turns any Base token into a plain-spoken AI brief that pulls
together its market data, holder distribution, and contract-security signals. You
pick a token, preview its chart, then hand it to the agent, which writes the
report right in the chat.

## Generate a report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a token">
    Pick a token from the live list (see [Discover and browse
    tokens](/user/tokens/discovery)), or paste a contract address to open that
    exact token's chart directly. Either way, the token's chart opens in the side
    panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the report">
    With the chart panel open, click the **research** button in its header. This
    opens the agent chat and asks for a report on that token. The agent writes a
    fresh report into the conversation from current data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Pasting a contract address is the most reliable way to research a brand-new or
low-cap token that hasn't reached the trending or AI-agent lists yet.
</Tip>

## What the report covers

The brief is written in four sections, in this order:

| Section | What's in it |
| --- | --- |
| **summary** | One or two sentences on what the token is and the headline read. |
| **market** | Traction signals: 24h volume relative to liquidity, momentum across the 1h vs 24h price change, holder count, and the market-cap to liquidity ratio (a thin float means higher dump risk). |
| **risk** | Liquidity depth, token age (newer is riskier), and holder concentration, factoring in the security and holder-distribution signals below. |
| **verdict** | A single bold line, `**relative risk: low \| medium \| high**`, followed by a one-clause reason (see [Reading the security findings](#reading-the-security-findings)). |

Alongside the market figures, the report draws on two extra signals:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Contract security" icon="shield-check">
    Safety flags: honeypot, blocked or cannot-sell, blacklist, buy/sell taxes,
    unverified source, and whether ownership is renounced.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Holder distribution" icon="users">
    Holder metrics: top-holder concentration, locked liquidity, and burn, used to
    gauge dump and rug risk.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The agent will not give financial advice or price predictions, and it never
prepares a transaction as part of a report.

<Note>
If a token was launched through a reputable launchpad's standard template, the
report treats contract-level rug and honeypot risk as lower, since templated
launches remove owner mint and blacklist backdoors. That does not remove market
risk like thin liquidity or holder concentration.
</Note>

## Reading the security findings

A few things to keep in mind when you read the security findings:

- **There is no single safety score.** The report never shows one number you can
  rank tokens by. Instead it surfaces the individual flags listed above and weighs
  them in the **risk** and **verdict** sections.
- **A missing flag does not mean "safe."** When there is no data for an
  indicator it is treated as *unknown*, not as a pass. If security data is
  unavailable for a contract entirely, the report says so rather than guessing.
- **Read the verdict as a relative gauge, not a guarantee.** **high** is reserved
  for hard red flags (honeypot, very high taxes, mintable or pausable ownership,
  a single wallet controlling most of supply). Ordinary speculative concerns like
  thin liquidity or a young token land at **low** or **medium**.

<Warning>
A clean report is not a safety guarantee. Onchain data can be stale, incomplete,
or absent, and a renounced, audit-clean contract can still lose value. Always do
your own checks before trading. See [Connect your wallet](/user/getting-started/wallet-connection)
before placing any trade.
</Warning>

## Saving and sharing a report

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sharing a token via a link" icon="link">
    Every token has a shareable link. Opening it switches to the Research tab and
    loads that token's chart panel, so a teammate can preview the chart and run
    their own fresh report with one click. Reports are generated on demand from
    current data, so the link points at the **token**, not at a frozen copy of
    your brief.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keeping a copy of the brief" icon="copy">
    The report is written into the agent conversation, so to keep a copy just
    copy the text out of the chat. The figures reflect the moment the report was
    generated, so re-run it whenever you need current numbers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why a report might not generate" icon="circle-alert">
    If nothing appears, check that the underlying services are enabled (the page
    surfaces the exact message). You may also need to wait a moment if you
    generate many reports in quick succession. Security and holder data degrade
    gracefully, if they're unavailable the report simply notes it and proceeds on
    market data alone.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
